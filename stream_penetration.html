This page is a companion to the paper above. It collects the simulation movies referenced in the draft, organised in the same logic as Sections 4–6 plus the convergence-test appendix. The text and figures of the paper are not duplicated here; please consult the manuscript for the physical analysis.

Simulation parameters

Each simulation is labelled R{rs}D{χ}P{P}M{M}, where the four control parameters are:

rs,f
Final stream radius in the post-equilibration CGM, in kpc.
χf ≡ ρs,f/ρh
Final density contrast between the cold stream and the hot CGM (after pressure equilibration).
P ≡ Ph/Ps,i
Initial pressure contrast between the hot CGM and the cold filamentary stream (CFM) before it has entered the halo.
Ms ≡ vs,i/ch
Mach number of the cold stream relative to the ambient CGM sound speed.

The fiducial run is R1D100P7M1: rs,f=1 kpc, χf=100, P=7, Ms=1.

What you are looking at in each movie

Every movie shows the same five horizontal panels, evolving in time, of a 3D cylindrical cold stream interacting with a hot CGM. From top to bottom:

  • nH — hydrogen number density (cm-3).
  • T — gas temperature (K).
  • P/kB — thermal pressure (K cm-3).
  • vy — velocity along the stream direction (km/s).
  • Mach number — local Mach number of the flow.

Spatial axes are in kpc, and time is reported in units of Lbox/vs. Regime tags below each movie reflect the classification used in the paper: coagulation fragmentation disruption borderline convergence test.

§5 — Effects of pressure contrast

Fixing rs,f=1 kpc and Ms=1, we vary the initial pressure contrast P=Ph/Ps,i across the same three density contrasts. A finite P drives an oblique shock that steepens into a bow shock at large contrasts; the dominant role of P is to amplify the transient mass and surface area during the restoring phase before pressure equilibrium is reached.

Appendix — Convergence tests

To verify that our results are not artefacts of finite resolution, the three fiducial-Mach, fiducial-pressure runs (rs,f=1 kpc; χf=50, 100, 300) are repeated at two additional refinement levels: L10 (lower) and L12 (higher). The standard production runs shown above use refinement level 11.
